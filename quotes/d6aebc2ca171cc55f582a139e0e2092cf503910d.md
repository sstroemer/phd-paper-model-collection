_Intuitive explanation of the complexity order of interior point algorithms._

## Quote(s)

> For a linear program in standard form [...] where the constraint matrix $A \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ (i.e., $m$ constraints and $n$ variables) has full row rank (which implies $n \geq m$), the best-known interior-point algorithms require $O(\sqrt{n} \log(1/\epsilon))$ iterations to achieve a precision of $\epsilon$.
